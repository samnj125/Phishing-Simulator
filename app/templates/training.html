How to spot a phishing email

Check the sender addressReal companies use their own domain. A "Microsoft" email from gmail.com or a misspelled domain is a red flag.
Hover before you clickAlways hover over links to see the real URL before clicking. If the domain looks wrong, don't click.
Urgency is a manipulation tacticPhrases like "Your account will be suspended" or "Act immediately" are designed to make you panic and skip verification.
When in doubt, go directInstead of clicking the link in the email, open your browser and type the website address yourself.
